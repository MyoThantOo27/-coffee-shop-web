History Of Coffee

Coffee's history traces back to 9th-century Ethiopia, where it was first discovered for its stimulating effects. By the 15th century, coffee was cultivated in Yemen and spread across the Arabian Peninsula. Coffeehouses became popular in the Ottoman Empire as social hubs. In the 17th century, European traders brought coffee to Europe, where it gained widespread popularity and fueled intellectual exchanges in coffeehouses. Through European colonization, coffee plantations spread to the Americas and Southeast Asia, making it a global commodity and cultural staple.
